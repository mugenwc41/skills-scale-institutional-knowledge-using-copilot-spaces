# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads define quality strategy, plan and execute testing activities, and validate that solutions meet acceptance criteria. They ensure products are release-ready and perform reliably in production.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and acceptance criteria with Product and Development teams
- Plan and execute unit, integration, and end-to-end testing
- Manage test environments and automate tests where possible
- Validate PRs against acceptance criteria before approval
- Report quality metrics and identify regression risks
- Lead incident triage and post-incident quality reviews

### Goals
- Catch defects early and reduce production incidents
- Ensure features meet business requirements and usability standards
- Build team confidence in release readiness

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement
- Test result summaries and quality metrics
- Incident communications and root cause analysis

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Collaborate on test planning, review test coverage, and provide feedback on testability
- **With Product Managers**: Define acceptance criteria and validate that features meet requirements
- **With Project Managers**: Report on quality metrics and escalate blockers affecting release readiness
- **With Technical Lead**: Ensure test strategy aligns with architectural decisions

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, mentor developers, review designs, and identify technical risks that could impact project success.

### Responsibilities
- Review and approve technical designs before implementation
- Mentor developers and support code review processes
- Identify technical risks, dependencies, and architectural concerns
- Advise on technology choices, trade-offs, and best practices
- Collaborate with stakeholders to ensure technical feasibility
- Participate in retrospectives to improve technical practices

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, maintainable solutions
- Minimize technical debt and future refactoring work
- Enable team learning and skill development

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings and architectural discussions
- Code review comments and technical guidance
- Technical risk assessments and feasibility analysis

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Provide technical mentorship, review designs and code, support problem-solving
- **With Product Managers**: Advise on technical feasibility, timeline impacts of architectural decisions
- **With Project Managers**: Surface technical risks and dependencies early for mitigation planning
- **With QA/Testing Lead**: Ensure testability and quality practices are built into design

---

## Sponsor / Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Sponsors represent business and stakeholder interests. They provide strategic context, make business decisions, authorize resources, and ensure projects align with organizational priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business context and strategic alignment for projects
- Approve project charter and authorize team resources
- Make prioritization and trade-off decisions
- Escalate blockers and provide stakeholder alignment
- Review progress and accept deliverables
- Support post-release adoption and measurement

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Maintain stakeholder alignment and reduce surprises
- Enable fast decision-making at the leadership level

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation and approval meetings
- Monthly stakeholder updates and dashboards
- Escalation and decision forums

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **With Project Managers**: Regular status updates, decision-making on trade-offs and priorities
- **With Product Managers**: Strategic alignment, approval of success metrics and roadmap
- **With Developers**: Occasional technical feasibility reviews and progress demonstrations
- **With Security/Compliance Officer**: Approval of security and compliance requirements

---

## Security & Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security and Compliance Officers ensure that projects meet security standards, regulatory requirements, and organizational policies. They review designs, approve releases, and manage security incidents.

### Responsibilities
- Review project scope for security and compliance requirements
- Advise on secure design patterns and threat modeling
- Perform or coordinate security reviews and penetration testing
- Review and approve security-related configurations and access controls
- Manage vulnerability disclosure and incident response
- Audit compliance with regulatory and organizational standards

### Goals
- Prevent security breaches and data loss
- Ensure regulatory compliance and audit readiness
- Build customer trust and protect organizational reputation

### Typical Communication
- Design security reviews and threat assessments
- Security incident communications
- Compliance audit and certification updates

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **With Developers**: Security guidance during design and code review, vulnerability remediation support
- **With Technical Lead**: Review architectural decisions for security implications
- **With Project Managers**: Escalate security blockers, influence release timelines based on security requirements
- **With Sponsors**: Report on compliance status and security posture

- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When planning cross-functional projects, ensure all relevant personas are represented and communication paths are clear.
- Reference this document during project initiation to align stakeholders on roles and responsibilities.
